PhD Linguistics Candidate

I am a PhD candidate of Linguistics and English Language (LEL) in the School of Philosophy, Psychology and Language Science (PPLS) at the University of Edinburgh.

  • Location: Edinburgh, Scotland OR Urbana, IL, USA
  • Pronouns: He/Him
  • Age: 28
  • Names: Józsi, Dódi, A-lóng

My PhD research revolves around language policy planning (LPP) of the languages of Taiwan. I am particularly interested in the way language status planning and corpus planning reflects and/or influences language use and language identity. My other interests include written forms of languages, language script planning, language ideology, education, language in education, language teaching, language maintenance, and language revitalisation.

PhD Project

My PhD project is a series of articles that attempt to look into multiple aspects of "mother-language" (local language) revitalisation and maintenance efforts in Taiwan.

Mandarin-plus to mandarin-inclusive

This opening part of my project attempts to synthesise previous analysis of language policy in Taiwan, as well as update information. It sets up the groundwork for understanding the rest of the project.

Standardisation of writing: the written forms of Tâi-gí

Writing is important to the users of some of the worlds languages, users of Tâi-gí is no exception. This part of my projects dives into some of the aspects of written Tâi-gí with and emphasis on the current debates and rhetoric on the written forms of Tâi-gí.
